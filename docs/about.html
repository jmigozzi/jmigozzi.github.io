About


I am an Urban Studies Foundation Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the School of Geography and the Environment at the University of Oxford.

My ongoing research investigates how digital technologies transform the real estate and finance industries by creating global real estate networks and reshaping urban housing markets. Most of my work focuses on post-apartheid South Africa.

I am particularly interested in credit scoring, real estate platforms (PropTech) and FinTech, and how such technologies interact with and renew segregation and wealth inequalities.

My research involves mixed methods framework that combines in-depth fieldwork based on expert interviews and participant-observation with computational analysis and mapping, using open source tools such as R or QGIS.

I also occasionally write or talk about rugby, unconditionally support the Rugby Club Toulonnais, and love improv’ and sports theatre.

Appointments and Education

As a Research Associate in Finance and Geography, I was previously part of the ERC project “Cities in Global Financial Network” led by Professor Dariusz Wójcik. Our team authored the first Atlas of Finance, to be published by Yale University Press in 2024.

Prior to joining the University of Oxford, I was a Lecturer in the Department of Geography at the Ecole Normale Supérieure in Paris.

I earned my MA from the Ecole Normale Supérieure de Lyon and my PhD from the University of Grenoble Alpes.

Affiliations

I am a Research Associate at the Research Labs Géographie-cités and PACTE, an Associate Member of the St Peter’s College, and a regular Visiting Scholar at the African Center for Cities at the University of Cape Town.
